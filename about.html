MAWOC

In a historic milestone for the University of Calabar, the Malabor World Cup (MAWOC) is set to debut with a groundbreaking 32-team format, marking a significant first in the university's sporting history. Under the visionary leadership of Diogo Sekav Abednego, affectionately known as Pele, the Director of Sport of the Student Union Government (SUG), MAWOC is poised to redefine sporting excellence and inclusivity within the university community. The decision to adopt a 32-team format for MAWOC underscores Unical's commitment to fostering a culture of participation and engagement among its students. By expanding the tournament to accommodate a larger number of teams, MAWOC opens doors for a more diverse range of athletes to showcase their talents on the grand stage. This unprecedented initiative not only amplifies the competitive spirit within the university but also provides a platform for nurturing budding talent and promoting grassroots sports development. With teams representing various departments, faculties, and interest groups, MAWOC promises to be a melting pot of athleticism, camaraderie, and school pride. As the University of Calabar prepares to host this monumental event, the stage is set for an exhilarating display of skill, passion, and sportsmanship. Through the collaborative efforts of Pele, the SUG, and the university administration, MAWOC is poised to leave an indelible mark on the sporting landscape of Unical, setting a new standard of excellence for years to come.
